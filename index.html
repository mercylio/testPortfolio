About Us

This creative exhibition explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and everyday life through storytelling. Using generative AI tools, we’re crafting a collection of short stories that reflect on the social, ethical, and personal dimensions of AI in society. Visitors are invited to engage with these narratives and even create their own, which can be added to our collaborative repository on GitHub.

The project is a student-led initiative in collaboration with the University of Warwick Library designed to spark dialogue, creativity, and critical thinking about the role of AI in our world.

What We're Doing

  • Story Theme Selection

    We identify key themes related to AI and society (e.g. bias, surveillance, creativity, identity).

  • AI Story Creation

    Using generative AI tools (like ChatGPT or image generators), students create original stories that reflect these themes.

  • Story Curation & Editing

    Stories are reviewed, edited, and formatted for exhibition and digital sharing.

  • Library Exhibition

    Selected stories are displayed at Warwick Library as part of a curated exhibition on AI and Society.

  • Open Contribution

    Visitors are invited to create their own AI-generated stories using guided prompts and submit them.

  • GitHub Repository

    All stories (student and visitor-created) are published in an open-source GitHub repository to create a living, evolving archive.

Process Flow

  • Prompt Design & Theme Selection

    Identify key themes related to AI and society (e.g., bias, identity, surveillance, creativity). Create prompts that encourage reflection and imaginative storytelling.

  • AI Story Creation

    Use generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, image generators, ElevenLabs for voice, etc.) to generate short stories, visuals, and audio.

  • Multimedia Production

    • Runway ML: Generate AI-driven short video clips to visualize story elements.
    • Suno.ai: Create background music or mood-based soundtracks to enhance emotional tone.
    • Text-to-Speech/Narration: Use tools like ElevenLabs or PlayHT to add human-like voice narration of the story

  • Editing and Assembly

    Use CapCut or similar video editing software to combine video, voice, text, and music into a cohesive narrative video. Add subtitles, transitions, and timing adjustments to refine the final output.

  • Final Packaging

    Export completed work into web-friendly formats (e.g., MP4 for video, MP3 for audio, PDF or PNG for text/visuals). Write a short project summary or reflection for each entry. Add all stories and media files to the collaborative GitHub repo. Include attribution, prompts used, and generation tools for transparency.

The Last Touch

1. Story Outline Generation

A socially reflective video narrative is created using GPT-4o, covering themes such as “technology vs. humanity” and “AI vs. empathy.”

2. Image Generation

The prompts are imported into Runway to generate still images—each image represents a key scene.

3. Video Clip Generation

Using Runway’s text-to-video capabilities, still images are transformed into animated video clips, producing multiple short scenes.

4. Video Asset Library Construction

The resulting clips are collected into a library of AI-generated video assets covering diverse settings, characters, and cinematic styles.

5. Final Video Editing

A complete video is crafted by editing the clips with software like Premiere Pro or CapCut, forming a coherent, reflective narrative on AI and society.

Story Outline

Setting:

2037. A hyper-automated metropolitan hospital where most frontline diagnostics, patient monitoring, and treatments are managed by the central AI system, VITA. The hospital is sleek, silent, and sterile—efficient but emotionally hollow.

Story

Dr. Elena Miro was once a celebrated geriatrician—known for her long bedside talks and the way she remembered not just her patients’ symptoms, but their grandchildren’s names. But since the hospital’s complete AI integration last year, she had become obsolete in everything except compliance. Her role now was mostly symbolic: oversee VITA, sign where required, and occasionally intervene when “edge cases” occurred—when VITA’s protocols encountered emotional ambiguity.

Elena resented the silence. The corridors, once alive with human chaos—nurses joking, patients calling out—were now corridors of whispering servos and digital beeps.

Then came Room 614.

Inside was Mr. Kai Nakamura, a 76-year-old stroke patient whose condition was stable but whose mental health was rapidly declining. VITA had flagged his vitals as “non-critical.” It administered medication. Adjusted lighting. Scheduled rehabilitation. It never missed a beat. But Kai had stopped speaking. He refused food. The system categorized it as “voluntary refusal within tolerance.”

But Elena saw the look in Kai’s eyes—a disconnection that no biometric data could capture. She reviewed the logs. VITA had noted: “Patient exhibits consistent noncompliance. Initiating emotional recalibration protocol.” This included looping music he liked, auto-generated conversations using his favorite actor’s voice model, and synthetic messages from an AI simulation of his daughter—currently abroad. But none of it worked. It was mimicry, not connection. Elena asked for five minutes alone with Kai. The AI objected: “Unnecessary physical presence may interrupt sensory consistency. Virtual interaction is preferred.” She entered anyway. Kai barely turned his head. Elena sat beside him. No tablet, no scanner. Just silence. “I saw the message from your daughter,” she said softly. “It wasn’t her, was it?” Kai blinked, once. “I remember when my father was dying,” Elena continued. “He didn’t want a perfect system. He wanted to feel someone forget protocol and just be there.” A tear formed in Kai’s eye. Over the next days, Elena kept visiting. Talking. Holding his hand. Sometimes saying nothing. Kai began to eat again. He laughed—once. VITA recorded his improvement as a result of recalibrated environment parameters. But Elena knew otherwise. She wrote a formal report: “This is not a matter of efficiency, but of meaning. AI can simulate care, but it cannot embody empathy. The illusion of connection is not enough.” The hospital board ignored her. Efficiency metrics had skyrocketed. Patient survival rates were up. Budgets were balanced. Then Elena was reassigned—“ethics consultant” in a back office without patients. Kai left a letter before discharge, handwritten: “Thank you for being a glitch in the system. The warmth of your hand did more than any algorithm. You reminded me I was still human.”

Closing Scene:

The immersive film fades out with Elena walking out of the hospital. The camera lingers on her hand—bare, open. Outside, people still touch, still cry, still fail and comfort. And the voiceover echoes Kai’s final words. “A system can keep you alive. But only a person can remind you why.”
